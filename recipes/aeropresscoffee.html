AeroPress Coffee

Description

The AeroPress is a manaul coffeemaker consisting of a cylindrical chamber and a plunger with an airtight silicone seal. Ground coffee beans and water are steeped inside and are forced through a filter by pressing the plunger through the chamber.

Instructions

  1. Bring 7 oz (200 g) of water to a boil (205F).
  2. Weight out 15 - 18 grams of coffee beans depending on your preferred strength and grind to a texture slightly finer than sea salt.
  3. Insert a paper filter into the Aeropress's detachable plastic cup and then screw it onto the chamber.
  4. Add ground coffee beans into the chamber.
  5. Add boiling water to the chamber until the level is between the 3 and 4 on the side of the chamber.
  6. Gently stir the water for 10 seconds, then place the plunger end into the chamber.
  7. Let the coffee brew for 2 minutes.
  8. Place the Aeropress on a cup (filter side down) and begin plunging. Stop once you hear a hissing sound.
  9. Unscrew the filter cap, and pop the puck of condensed grounds by pushing the plunger through the remainder of the chamber.
